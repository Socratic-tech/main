Common Misconceptions

"Everyone is a little ADHD"

False. While everyone is forgetful, for ADHD, it is more severe, frequent, and debilitating.

"They could do it if they wanted to"

It isn't a lack of will. Executive Dysfunction means: "I want to do it, but I can't."

Visual & Predictable Timers

Use timers that show the passage of time (like a Red Wing timer) or song timers (e.g., "Jackson 5's ABC" is exactly 3 minutes).

The Binder Clip Trick

Standard bookmarks fall out. Use binder clips to lock planners and notebooks to the exact "current" page for instant access.

Minimally Viable Product

Combat perfectionism/paralysis by defining the absolute minimum required for a passing grade (e.g., "Title + 4 slides").

Transition "Stretch"

Use neutral activities (GoNoodle, stretch breaks) when moving from a preferred task to a non-preferred one.

Math & Writing Adaptations

Math Alignment

Illegible handwriting often ruins math performance. Use these tactical fixes:

  • Graph Paper: Use 1/2" or 1/4" squares to force number alignment.
  • 90-Degree Rotation: Turn standard lined paper sideways so the lines act as vertical columns for math problems.

Writing Logic

Help students bypass the "blank page" fear:

  • Sticky Note Drafting: One idea per sticky note. Move them around on a wall to organize paragraphs before writing a single sentence.
  • Speech Bubbles: Students find "What do you think about this?" in a bubble less intimidating than a formal essay prompt.
