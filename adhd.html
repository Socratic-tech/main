Just the Tip of the Iceberg: ADHD Toolkit

Strategies for Educators by Mary Phillips, Elementary STEAM Teacher

1. Understanding the ADHD Brain

NEURODEVELOPMENTAL

The Regulation Challenge

ADHD is not a lack of knowledge; it's a disorder of regulation and balance[cite: 26].

  • Interest-Based Brain: Decision-making is driven by novelty, interest, or challenge, not just importance[cite: 14, 28].
  • Dopamine Seeking: The brain is constantly looking for dopamine and glucose to function[cite: 26].
  • Executive Dysfunction: Affects time management, organization, and emotional control[cite: 14, 27].
MYTH BUSTING

Changing the Narrative

  • "Lazy" vs "Can't": Executive dysfunction often sounds like "I want to do it, but I can't"[cite: 33].
  • Masking: Students (especially girls) may appear fine while struggling internally to meet social norms[cite: 31].
  • Avoid Superlatives: Telling a perfectionist to "always do your best" can be anxiety-inducing[cite: 49].

2. Actionable Classroom Hacks

Organization & Focus

  • World’s Easiest System: Use a 2-pocket folder labeled "Home" and "Return to School"[cite: 44].
  • Binder Clips: Use them to help students find the next blank page in a notebook instantly.
  • Say it in 3: Provide sticky notes with no more than 3 steps at a time to avoid overwhelm[cite: 45, 46].
  • Visual Timers: Use timers that show the physical passage of time[cite: 40].

Academic Modifications

  • The MVP Approach: Define the "Minimally Viable Product"—the least amount of work required for mastery[cite: 49, 51].
  • Sticky Note Writing: One idea per sticky note. Organize them on a wall before drafting.
  • Math Clarity: Use graph paper or rotate lined paper 90 degrees to keep numbers in vertical columns[cite: 59].
  • Rethink Listening: Allow doodling or standing; SLANT doesn't work for every neurodivergent student.

The "Shock" Reset

When students face big emotional reactions or "brain lock":

  • Sour Candy: A sour taste can help re-engage the thinking brain.
  • Cold Water: Splashing the face with cold water serves as a sensory reset.
  • Validate First: Name the emotion and validate it before trying to solve the problem.
