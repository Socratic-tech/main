Regulation & Balance

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder rooted in genetic and environmental triggers. It is characterized by synapses that underperform in regulating dopamine and glucose.

Common Misconceptions

"Everyone is a little ADHD"

False. While everyone is forgetful, for ADHD, it is more severe, frequent, and debilitating.

"They could do it if they wanted to"

It isn't a lack of will. Executive Dysfunction means: "I want to do it, but I can't."

Visual & Predictable Timers

Use timers that show the physical passage of time, or use song timers (e.g., "Jackson 5's ABC" is exactly 3 minutes).

The Binder Clip Trick

Standard bookmarks fall out. Use binder clips to lock planners and notebooks to the exact "current" page for instant access.

Minimally Viable Product

Combat perfectionism and task paralysis by defining the absolute minimum required for a passing grade.

Transition "Stretch"

Use neutral activities (GoNoodle, stretch breaks) when moving from a preferred task to a non-preferred one.

Math & Writing Adaptations

Math Alignment

Illegible handwriting often ruins math performance. Use these tactical fixes:

  • ‚úì Graph Paper: Use 1/2" or 1/4" squares to force number alignment.
  • ‚úì 90-Degree Rotation: Turn standard lined paper sideways so the lines act as vertical columns for math problems.

Writing Logic

Help students bypass the "blank page" fear:

  • ‚úì Sticky Note Drafting: One idea per sticky note. Move them around on a wall to organize paragraphs before writing a single sentence.
  • ‚úì Speech Bubbles: Students find "What do you think about this?" in a bubble less intimidating than a formal essay prompt.
