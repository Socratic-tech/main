The ADHD Brain Mechanics

Understanding why the brain "won't" vs. "can't"[cite: 152].

NEUROBIOLOGY

Regulation & Dopamine

ADHD is a disorder of regulation and balance, not lack of effort[cite: 143].

  • Dopamine Seeking: Brain synapses underperform, leading to a constant search for dopamine and glucose (novelty, interest, or chocolate!)[cite: 143].
  • Hyperfocus: Often called "Flow," it allows for massive information absorption but distorts the sense of time[cite: 162].
  • Executive Function Delay: Social/emotional skills can be delayed by as much as 3 years compared to peers[cite: 30].
COGNITIVE

Interest-Based Decisions

ADHD brains prioritize tasks based on Interest, not Importance[cite: 145].

Below the Surface (The Iceberg)

The visible symptoms—hyperactivity and inattention—are just the tip. What's hidden is more complex[cite: 63].

  • Masking: Especially common in girls; hiding ADHD symptoms to fit societal norms[cite: 149].
  • 2E (Twice Exceptional): High performance/giftedness paired with neurodivergence[cite: 148].
  • Justice Sensitivity: Intense feelings of unfairness that can look like "tattling"[cite: 156].

Myths to Retire[cite: 141]:

"Everyone is a little ADHD."

False. While everyone forgets things, for ADHD, these experiences are more severe, more frequent, and more debilitating[cite: 141].

The Classroom Hacks

World's Easiest Org

A simple 2-pocket folder system:

  • Pocket 1: Keep at Home
  • Pocket 2: Return to School

Binder Clips [cite: 39]

Unlike bookmarks, binder clips don't fall out. Use them to clip current pages in planners or notebooks for instant access[cite: 39].

Label Everything [cite: 37]

Use clear containers. Short-term memory issues make items "invisible" if they aren't in plain sight[cite: 37, 38].

Academic Specifics

Math & Writing Hacks

  • 90-Degree Rotation: Turn lined paper sideways so the lines become columns to keep math problems aligned[cite: 59].
  • Graph Paper for Math: Use 1/2 inch or 1/4 inch squares to combat illegible alignment[cite: 59].
  • Sticky Note Drafting: Record one idea per sticky note. Organize them on a wall into paragraphs before writing[cite: 58].

The Reading Tracker [cite: 39]

Provide paper copies and insist on finger-tracking or using a straight edge to follow along. This provides a tactile reminder of the current task[cite: 39].

Behavioral & Emotional Reset

The "Sensory Shock" Reset

To re-engage a child's thinking brain after a big reaction:

  • ⚡ Offer extremely sour candy.
  • ❄️ Have student splash face with cold water.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

Combat perfectionism and "Task Paralysis" by defining the absolute least amount of work required for a passing grade.

Example: "4 slides, 1 image each, name on title" vs. "A perfect 10-slide deck".
